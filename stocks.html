Stock Exchange

Index stocks track raw materials and drift with supply and demand. Player IPOs are shares in actual player-owned shops. Owning shares earns you a cut of that shop's weekly revenue.

Prices update weekly. Once the bridge is live, live data will appear here.

What are stocks?

Stocks let you invest in the server economy without running a shop yourself. Buy shares in a profitable shop and you receive a weekly dividend from its revenue. Share prices go up if the shop does well and down if trade drops off.

Index stocks like IRON and DIAMOND track the raw material market. They are a lower-risk way to hold value and speculate on price movements.

Taking your shop public (IPO)

  1. Your QuickShop must have at least £500 in weekly revenue.
  2. Run /ipo <ticker> <shares> <price> in-game to list your shares.
  3. 10% of your weekly shop revenue is distributed to shareholders as dividend every Monday.
  4. You keep control of your shop as long as you hold more than 50% of shares.

Think carefully before going public. Shareholders will be watching your revenue. If your shop goes quiet, the share price drops and people will notice.
